Meta has introduced a paywall for its Ray-Ban smart glasses' Conversation Focus feature, limiting its use to three hours a month for free accounts. The feature, which amplifies the voice of the person you're speaking to in noisy environments, can be used for up to 15 hours a month with a Meta One Premium subscription. This move has sparked frustration among users, who feel that a feature they've already paid for through the purchase of the glasses is being taken away.

## What happened
The Conversation Focus feature was showcased at Meta Connect last year and is essentially an audio mixer for real-life conversations. It uses the glasses' microphones to pick up the voice of the person you're looking at, separates their voice from background noise, and plays it back to you through the glasses' speakers. However, Meta has now introduced a rate limit for this feature, limiting its use to three hours a month for free accounts.

The feature is handled entirely on-device, so it's unclear why Meta has chosen to paywall it. The company has stated that the subscription is optional and that most people will use Conversation Focus without hitting the monthly limit. However, this move has still sparked frustration among users, who feel that a feature they've already paid for is being taken away.

The paywall has been introduced as part of Meta's efforts to monetize its AI features. The company has been investing heavily in AI research and development, and is now looking for ways to recoup its costs. However, the decision to paywall the Conversation Focus feature has been met with criticism from users, who feel that it's unfair to limit access to a feature that they've already paid for.

## Why it matters
The paywall on the Conversation Focus feature has significant implications for users who rely on this feature. For people with hearing troubles, this feature can be a vital tool for communicating in noisy environments. By limiting access to this feature, Meta is potentially excluding these users from being able to use the glasses to their full potential.

The decision to paywall the Conversation Focus feature also raises questions about the future of Meta's AI features. If the company is willing to limit access to a feature that's already been paid for, what's to stop it from doing the same with other features in the future? This could have significant implications for users who are invested in the Meta ecosystem and are relying on these features to enhance their daily lives.
