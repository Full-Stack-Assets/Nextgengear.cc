Honor Warren, the 18-year-old daughter of actress Jessica Alba and producer Cash Warren, is stepping into the film industry, not as an actress like her mother, but as a writer and director. She recently shared a clip from her short film, "Unsaid," on Instagram, citing classic French cinema as inspiration. This career milestone arrives amidst significant personal changes for the family, as her parents finalized their divorce in March 2026 after separating in 2024. Her foray into filmmaking marks a new chapter for the family, highlighting a generational shift in creative pursuits.

## What happened
Honor Warren, at 18 years old, publicly unveiled her directorial talents by sharing a clip from her short film, "Unsaid," on Instagram on June 24. She captioned the post, confirming she both wrote and directed the piece, and noted that a particular scene served as an "ode" to Jean-Luc Godard's iconic film, "Breathless." This debut signals her clear intention to pursue a career behind the camera within the entertainment industry.

This personal milestone for Honor occurs in the wake of her parents' separation and subsequent divorce. Jessica Alba and Cash Warren, who met in 2004 and married in 2008, announced their split in January 2025, with Alba filing for divorce in February 2025. The divorce was finalized in March 2026, with the former couple agreeing to joint legal and physical custody of their three children: Honor, Haven (14), and Hayes (8).
