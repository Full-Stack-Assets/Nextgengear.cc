## What happened
The FIFA World Cup 2026, running from June 11 to July 19, marks a significant expansion, now featuring 48 teams and a total of 104 matches hosted across the United States, Canada, and Mexico. This new format introduces a Round of 32, a single-elimination stage that runs from June 28 to July 3, preceding the traditional Round of 16.

All matches are broadcast on FOX or FOX Sports channels. For those preferring digital access, live streaming is available through the FOX One and FOX Sports apps. Additionally, free viewing options include FIFA+ and select YouTube streams, providing multiple avenues for fans to tune in.

The tournament structure includes a group stage where 48 teams are divided into 12 groups of four, followed by the newly introduced Round of 32, the Round of 16, Quarterfinals, and Semi-finals, all culminating in the Final championship match. Each knockout stage match, if tied after 90 minutes, proceeds to 30 minutes of extra time, and then to a penalty shootout if the tie persists.

## Why it matters
The expanded 48-team format fundamentally changes the tournament's scale and dynamics, offering more games and a longer viewing period for fans. The introduction of the Round of 32 means an additional knockout stage, intensifying the competition earlier and providing more high-stakes matches. This also impacts team strategies and player endurance throughout the extended schedule.

For viewers, the availability of matches across both traditional broadcast and multiple streaming platforms ensures broad accessibility. This flexibility caters to diverse viewing habits, from watching at home on a TV to streaming on mobile devices, making it easier for a global audience to follow their favorite teams and players. The inclusion of free streaming options further democratizes access to the world's biggest soccer event.

## How to think about it
When planning your World Cup viewing, consider your primary access method. If you have cable or antenna access, FOX and FOX Sports are your go-to channels. For cord-cutters or those on the move, the FOX One and FOX Sports apps offer comprehensive streaming. Explore FIFA+ or YouTube for free options, but be aware that specific match availability may vary. Keep an eye on the tournament schedule to follow the progression through the group stage, Round of 32, and subsequent knockout rounds, ensuring you understand how teams advance and are eliminated.
