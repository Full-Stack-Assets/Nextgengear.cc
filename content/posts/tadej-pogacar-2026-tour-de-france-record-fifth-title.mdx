The 113th Tour de France is set to commence, and all eyes are on Tadej Pogacar as he embarks on a journey that could etch his name deeper into cycling history. Having already claimed victory in 2020, 2021, 2024, and 2025, the Slovenian sensation is now targeting an unprecedented fifth title, a feat that would place him alongside the sport's most legendary figures. This year's race, kicking off in Barcelona, promises three weeks of grueling competition, dramatic mountain stages, and intense rivalries as Pogacar seeks to cement his legacy.

<Callout type="takeaway">
Tadej Pogacar is the clear favorite to win the 2026 Tour de France and achieve a record-equaling fifth title, but faces strong competition and the inherent unpredictability of the race.
</Callout>

## What happened
Fresh off a dominant season that included winning the Tour de Suisse in June, Tadej Pogacar (UAE Team Emirates-XRG) enters the 2026 Tour de France as the undisputed favorite. His versatility across all disciplines, particularly his climbing prowess and individual time trial speed, makes him a formidable contender. The 2026 edition features 21 stages, beginning in Barcelona, marking the 27th time the race has started outside France.

However, Pogacar is not without challengers. Jonas Vingegaard (Visma-Lease a Bike), a two-time winner in 2022 and 2023, has returned to form after injury setbacks and recently triumphed in the Giro d'Italia by over five minutes. Isaac del Toro, Pogacar's teammate, has also shown strong form with victories at the UAE Tour and Tirreno-Adriatico, positioning him as a potential podium finisher. Other notable riders include French debutant Paul Seixas (Decathlon-CMA CGM), a rising 19-year-old talent, and Belgian Remco Evenepoel (Red Bull-Bora-Hansgrohe). British hopes for stage wins rest with riders like Tom Pidcock, who leads Pinarello-Q36.5 in their debut, while Oscar Onley is a notable absentee due to injury.

## Why it matters
Pogacar's pursuit of a fifth Tour de France title is more than just another victory; it's a quest for sporting immortality. Equaling the record held by Bernard Hinault, Eddy Merckx, Jacques Anquetil, and Miguel Indurain would solidify his status as one of the greatest cyclists of all time. This historical significance adds immense pressure and excitement to every stage, captivating fans worldwide.

Beyond Pogacar's personal ambition, the race holds significant implications for his rivals and national pride. Vingegaard's return to top form sets the stage for a compelling showdown, a continuation of their intense rivalry. For France, the emergence of young talent like Paul Seixas offers a glimmer of hope for a future home-grown winner, ending a decades-long wait. The performance of British riders also garners considerable attention, with Tom Pidcock eyeing stage victories and a potential top-10 general classification finish.

## How to think about it
When following the 2026 Tour de France, consider it not just a series of individual stages, but a grand narrative unfolding over three weeks. Focus on the strategic battles within the peloton, the alliances and rivalries that emerge, and how riders manage their energy across punishing mountain ascents and high-speed descents. Appreciate the historical context of Pogacar's record attempt, but also keep an eye on the emerging talents and the domestiques who play crucial roles. The Tour is a testament to human endurance and tactical brilliance, where every second and every decision can alter the course of history.

<Callout type="warning">
Do not underestimate the unpredictable nature of the Tour de France. Crashes, illness, or a single bad day in the mountains can derail even the strongest favorite. While Pogacar is heavily favored, the race is far from a foregone conclusion, and upsets are always a possibility.
</Callout>
