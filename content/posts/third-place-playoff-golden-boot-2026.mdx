The World Cup’s third‑place play‑off isn’t just a consolation match; it directly influences the coveted Golden Boot race. FIFA confirms that any goal scored in this fixture adds to a player’s tournament tally. With France and England set to meet in Saturday’s match, Mbappé, Dembele, Kane and Bellingham remain in contention despite their teams’ semi‑final exits. The decision matters because the Golden Boot can be decided by a single goal or assist in the final days of the tournament.

<Callout type="takeaway">Goals in the third‑place match still count toward a player’s Golden Boot tally.</Callout>

## What happened
Goals scored by players in the World Cup’s third‑place play‑off will count towards a player’s goal tally and the race for the Golden Boot, according to FIFA regulations. As of the semi‑final stage, Kylian Mbappé and Lionel Messi sit joint‑top with eight goals each, while England’s Harry Kane and Jude Bellingham have six each, keeping the race wide open.

Historical precedent shows the impact of the match: Thomas Müller (2010), Davor Suker (1998), Salvatore Schillaci (1990) and Leonidas (1938) all needed their third‑place goals to clinch the award. Others like Grzegorz Lato (1974), Eusebio (1966) and Just Fontaine (1958) also scored in the fixture, though they would have won regardless.

The upcoming England‑France showdown could be decisive, as both managers may field strong line‑ups to give their strikers a final scoring opportunity before the tournament concludes.

## Why it matters
Counting third‑place goals preserves the integrity of the Golden Boot by rewarding performance across the entire competition, not just the knockout rounds. It also adds strategic depth: coaches must weigh the risk of resting key players against the potential boost to a striker’s tally. Fans gain an extra narrative thread, keeping the third‑place match relevant and viewership high.

## How to think about it
When evaluating the Golden Boot race, factor in the remaining match schedule for each contender. A player whose team reaches the final has fewer opportunities to add goals than one playing in the third‑place match. Consider assist counts and minutes played, as FIFA uses assists as the first tie‑breaker and minutes as the second. For fantasy football participants, monitor squad announcements for the third‑place fixture; a starting striker could be a low‑cost, high‑upside pick.
