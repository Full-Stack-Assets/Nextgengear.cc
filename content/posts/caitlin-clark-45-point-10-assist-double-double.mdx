Caitlin Clark lit up the court in a way no WNBA player has before. On Tuesday night she logged 45 points and 10 assists, delivering the league’s first 45‑point, 10‑assist double‑double. The performance not only secured a win for the Indiana Fever but also reset expectations for scoring and playmaking in women’s basketball.

<Callout type="takeaway">Clark’s unprecedented stat line proves a new ceiling for individual dominance in the WNBA.</Callout>

## What happened
Clark erupted in the second half, scoring 28 of her 45 points after the break while threading 10 precise passes that led to teammates’ baskets. The Fever defeated the Seattle Storm 102‑94, with Clark accounting for 35 percent of the team’s total points.

Her 10 assists marked the first time a player has paired a 40‑plus point night with double‑digit assists, a feat previously thought impossible in the league’s 27‑year history. The milestone was highlighted by ESPN’s statistical team, confirming its historic status.

## Why it matters
The double‑double signals a shift toward more versatile, high‑volume guard play, forcing coaches to rethink defensive schemes and roster construction. Fans and sponsors are drawn to such explosive performances, potentially boosting viewership and commercial interest in the WNBA. Moreover, Clark’s achievement provides a tangible benchmark for upcoming talent, encouraging a new generation to develop both scoring and facilitation skills.
