Justin Poehnelt, a Google engineer, was fired for creating a viral Google Workspace CLI. The tool, which gives users and AI agents unified access to Google Drive, Gmail, Calendar, and every other Workspace API, was built in his role on the Workspace Developer Relations team. Poehnelt spent nearly seven years at Google before being let go.

<Callout type="takeaway">Google fired an engineer for creating a viral Google Workspace CLI, sparking debate about the company's approach to innovation and disruption.</Callout>

## What happened
Justin Poehnelt built a Google Workspace CLI that went viral, hitting number one on Hacker News and gaining thousands of GitHub stars. The tool was designed for both humans and AI agents and covered the full Google Workspace surface. Poehnelt was on the Workspace DevRel team, where building open-source layers and abstractions over Google APIs was a regular part of his job. Two days before he was fired, Google announced at Google Cloud Next that an official Workspace CLI was in development.

The controversy centered on the Google logo and brand colors appearing on the GitHub repository, which Poehnelt says was a policy violation. However, he believes the real reason for his termination was the fear of disruption that the tool represented. Poehnelt's read on the situation is that Workspace and certain leaders were afraid of being disrupted by the tool and the broader implications of AI agents for the company's business.

## Why it matters
The firing of Justin Poehnelt has sparked debate about Google's approach to innovation and disruption. The company has been working through what AI agents mean for its Workspace business, and the incident highlights the challenges of navigating this transition. The fact that Google announced an official Workspace CLI just two days before Poehnelt's termination raises questions about the company's priorities and commitment to innovation.
