Gamers looking for a cutting-edge display now have an even more affordable option as LG's UltraGear 27GX700A-B 1440p OLED gaming monitor has seen a significant price reduction. This 27-inch display, known for its vibrant visuals and high performance, is currently available at one of its lowest prices since its release last August. The drop reflects a broader trend of decreasing costs for advanced OLED gaming panels, making high-end features more accessible to a wider audience. This particular model boasts LG's fourth-generation WOLED technology, promising superior brightness and contrast that rivals even QD-OLED competitors.

<Callout type="takeaway">The LG UltraGear 27GX700A-B 1440p OLED gaming monitor is now available at a near-record low price, making advanced OLED technology more accessible for PC and console gamers.</Callout>

## What happened
The LG UltraGear 27GX700A-B, a 27-inch 1440p gaming monitor launched in August, has seen its price fall to as low as $484.99 at Amazon, with LG offering it for $499.99. This marks a substantial reduction from its original $599.99 retail price, positioning it as an attractive option for those seeking premium display technology without the premium cost typically associated with OLED. This particular model utilizes LG's Primary RGB Tandem panels, built with fourth-generation WOLED technology.

This advanced panel design delivers enhanced brightness and punchier contrast compared to earlier iterations, putting its visual performance on par with some QD-OLED displays. Beyond its stunning visuals, the 27GX700A-B is packed with features crucial for competitive gaming, including a blazing 280Hz refresh rate and a near-instant 0.03-millisecond response time. Connectivity is robust, offering twin HDMI 2.1 ports, a DisplayPort 1.4 jack, multiple USB-A ports for peripherals, and a dedicated headphone jack.

## Why it matters
The continued downward trend in OLED gaming monitor prices is a significant development for the gaming hardware market. While other PC components like RAM and storage have seen price increases, the accessibility of cutting-edge display technology like OLED is expanding. This means gamers no longer have to compromise as much on visual quality or performance when building or upgrading their setups. The LG UltraGear 27GX700A-B's price drop specifically opens up high-fidelity 1440p gaming to a broader segment of PC and console users whose systems are optimized for this resolution.

The integration of AMD FreeSync Premium Pro and Nvidia G-Sync compatibility ensures smooth, tear-free gameplay across a wide range of frame rates, adapting to the output of various graphics cards. This makes the monitor a versatile choice for a diverse gaming audience, from casual players to esports enthusiasts who demand fluidity and responsiveness. The availability of such advanced features at a more competitive price point pushes the entire display market forward, encouraging innovation and better value for consumers.
