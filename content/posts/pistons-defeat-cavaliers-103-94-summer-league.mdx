The Detroit Pistons rolled past the Cleveland Cavaliers 103-94 in Las Vegas during the NBA Summer League on July 12, 2026. The game showcased a balanced offensive attack and disciplined defense from Detroit. Rookie Chaz Lanier sparked the Pistons with a timely three‑pointer that helped swing momentum. For the Cavaliers, the loss exposed gaps in perimeter shooting and rebounding. The result matters because summer‑league performances often foreshadow roster moves and player development paths.

## What happened
The Pistons posted a 103‑94 victory, outscoring the Cavaliers in the second half after a 12‑point run sparked by Lanier’s three‑pointer. Detroit’s bench contributed 38 points, while the starters combined for 65, demonstrating depth across the roster. Cleveland struggled to answer, shooting just 42% from the field and committing 14 turnovers.
