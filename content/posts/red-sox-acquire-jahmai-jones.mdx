Jahmai Jones arrived in Boston on Tuesday as the Red Sox completed a post‑All‑Star break deal with Detroit, swapping the 28‑year‑old outfielder/DH for a player to be named later. Jones has struggled this season, posting a .137 batting average with two doubles, two homers and seven RBIs in 57 games for the Tigers. Boston hopes the hitter‑friendly confines of Fenway Park can spark a turnaround for the right‑handed bat. The move also gives the Sox a fresh option to balance a lineup that leans heavily left‑handed.

## What happened
The Red Sox announced the acquisition of Jahmai Jones, an outfielder/DH, from the Detroit Tigers in exchange for a player to be named later. Jones was designated for assignment by Detroit on July 9 after a season‑long slump, hitting just .137 with limited power. Boston immediately optioned outfielder Nate Eaton to Triple‑A Worcester to make room for Jones, who is expected to be active for the upcoming doubleheader against the Rays.

Jones entered the 2025 season as a solid platoon player for Detroit, posting a .287 average with 11 doubles, a triple, seven home runs and a .937 OPS over 72 games. He also contributed in the postseason, going 1‑for‑2 with a double and an RBI in four playoff games. The Red Sox view his prior success and right‑handed swing as a potential fit for Fenway’s park factors.

## Why it matters
Boston’s current outfield features three left‑handed hitters—Jarren Duran, Wilyer Abreu and Masataka Yoshida—leaving limited options against right‑handed pitching. Adding a right‑handed bat like Jones provides a strategic platoon advantage, especially in late‑inning matchups. Moreover, the trade signals the Sox’s willingness to tweak the roster mid‑season to maximize offensive production in a park known for boosting right‑handed power.
