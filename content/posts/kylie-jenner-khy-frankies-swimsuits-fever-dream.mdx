Summer 2026 just got a style boost as Kylie Jenner partners once more with Khy and cult‑favorite Frankies Swimsuits for the Fever Dream swimwear line. The collection, co‑designed by Jenner and founder Francesca Aiello, drops on July 14 after an exclusive preview in ELLE. Its vintage‑inspired silhouettes and bold patterns aim to capture the glamour of old Hollywood while staying firmly on‑trend. For shoppers hunting the season’s must‑have beachwear, the collaboration promises ultra‑flattering cuts that could dominate resort wardrobes.

<Callout type="takeaway">The Fever Dream line blends vintage Hollywood glamour with modern swim trends, setting the summer’s style benchmark.</Callout>

## What happened
The partnership reunites Jenner with Frankies Swimsuits founder Francesca Aiello, who noted that both drew inspiration from their personal closets and Los Angeles’ beach‑to‑hill vibe. Together they crafted a second‑act collection that mixes the brand’s playful, feminine ethos with Jenner’s iconic sense of style.

The line features the brand’s signature Cloud Cups in the ultra‑feminine Cherish and Camila tops, bold leopard, cobra and camo prints, and solid colorways like pink. All pieces are slated for a July 14 launch, with ELLE offering the first exclusive look.

## Why it matters
Fever Dream reinforces the power of celebrity‑driven collaborations to set seasonal trends, especially in the competitive swimwear market. By marrying vintage Hollywood cues with contemporary cuts, the collection raises the bar for design ambition and could influence other brands to pursue similar high‑glam aesthetics. For consumers, the partnership promises a blend of brand cachet and runway‑ready looks that justify premium price points.
