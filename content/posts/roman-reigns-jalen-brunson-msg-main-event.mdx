Saturday Night’s Main Event turned Madison Square Garden into a crossover arena where WWE and NBA stars collided. World Heavyweight Champion Roman Reigns teamed with 2026 NBA Finals MVP Jalen Brunson to repel challenges from Seth Rollins and Tyrese Haliburton. The night also saw Fatal Influence capture the Women’s Tag Team Championship and CM Punk defend the Undisputed WWE title. These outcomes signal a growing blend of sports entertainment and mainstream athletics, expanding the audience for future events.

## What happened
Roman Reigns and Jalen Brunson faced off against Seth Rollins and Tyrese Haliburton in a high‑stakes tag match, emerging victorious after a series of near‑falls and a decisive finish by Brunson. The win kept Reigns’ World Heavyweight Championship reign intact while giving Brunson his first major WWE spotlight.

In the women’s division, Lainey Reid and Fallon Henley of Fatal Influence dethroned Paige and Brie Bella to become the new WWE Women’s Tag Team Champions, delivering a hard‑hitting performance that earned a standing ovation. Meanwhile, Undisputed WWE Champion CM Punk and Cody Rhodes defeated Gunther and Sami Zayn in a contested bout, and Danhausen secured a No Disqualification win over JD McDonagh with assistance from Karl‑Anthony Towns.

The undercard featured United States Champion Trick Williams defending his title in a non‑title match against Laredo Kid, where Williams used his power advantage to retain after a brutal Trick Shot.

## Why it matters

The title changes, especially in the women’s tag division, refresh storylines and give emerging talent a platform, while cross‑promotional appearances by athletes like Karl‑Anthony Towns signal a willingness to experiment with guest stars. This strategy could boost viewership numbers and advertising revenue, but it also risks alienating purist fans who prefer traditional wrestling narratives.
