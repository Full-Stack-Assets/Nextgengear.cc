While Prime Day is often associated with discounts on tech gadgets and smart home devices, it has increasingly become a prime time for entertainment deals. This year, several major streaming platforms, including Apple TV, HBO, Peacock, and Paramount Plus, have been offering nearly free two-month trials for just a dollar a month. Today marks the absolute last day to take advantage of these significant savings, allowing subscribers to binge-watch popular series and movies throughout the summer for a minimal cost.

## What happened
Amazon Prime Day, known for its tech and gadget deals, has also featured significant discounts on streaming service subscriptions. For the duration of the event, and specifically on its final day, major platforms like Peacock, Paramount Plus, BritBox, and MGM Plus have offered trials for as low as $1 per month for two months. To access these promotions, users must sign up through an existing Amazon Prime account, linking the deal directly to their Prime membership.

Beyond the $1 trials, other services presented substantial savings. Apple TV was available for $6 for two months, providing a half-off opportunity to catch up on its exclusive series. HBO offered an annual ad-free subscription at over 30 percent off its regular price. Additionally, anime fans could secure Crunchyroll's Fan plan for $60 a year or the Mega Fan plan for $84 a year, both representing better-than-usual Prime Day discounts on annual subscriptions.

## Why it matters
These expiring Prime Day streaming deals represent a significant opportunity for Amazon Prime members to expand their entertainment options at a fraction of the usual cost. For a minimal investment, subscribers can access a vast library of content, from critically acclaimed dramas on HBO and Apple TV to popular shows on Paramount Plus and niche content on BritBox or MGM Plus. This allows users to sample services they might have been curious about, or simply enjoy a summer of binge-watching without committing to full-price subscriptions immediately.

The short-term nature of many of these trials—often two months for $1—makes them ideal for seasonal viewing, such as during summer breaks. It also helps consumers avoid subscription fatigue by offering a low-cost, low-commitment way to explore new shows and movies. Missing today's deadline means foregoing these specific, limited-time prices, which are often better than standard introductory offers.

## How to think about it
When considering these last-minute streaming deals, approach them strategically. First, identify which services host the specific shows or movies you've been wanting to watch. With trials often lasting two months, you have ample time to catch up on a few key series. Second, and crucially, set a calendar reminder a few days before your trial period ends to either cancel the subscription or decide if the service is worth its full monthly price. This prevents unexpected charges. Finally, remember these deals are tied to your Amazon Prime account, so ensure you're logged in before signing up. This is a chance to cheaply explore new content, not to inadvertently accumulate new recurring bills.

<Callout type="warning">
  Be vigilant about cancellation dates. These trials automatically convert to full-price subscriptions if not canceled before the promotional period ends, so mark your calendars!
</Callout>
