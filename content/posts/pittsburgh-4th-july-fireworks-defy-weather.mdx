## What happened
Reports indicated that Pittsburgh's annual 4th of July fireworks celebration faced significant weather challenges. Forecasts included both potential storms and high temperatures, raising concerns about the event's feasibility and public comfort. Despite these environmental considerations, local authorities and event organizers made the decision to proceed with the planned fireworks display over the city.

The successful execution of the event meant that thousands of spectators were able to gather and enjoy the patriotic spectacle. The decision to move forward, even under adverse weather predictions, highlighted a commitment to maintaining a cherished civic tradition for the Pittsburgh community.
