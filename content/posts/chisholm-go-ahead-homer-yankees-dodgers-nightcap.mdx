The Yankees snapped a doubleheader loss with a dramatic eighth‑inning home run from Jazz Chisholm Jr. after Aaron Boone’s lineup struggled early. Chisholm’s blast off Dodgers reliever Evan Phillips turned a 1‑1 tie into a 2‑1 victory at Yankee Stadium. The win not only evened the split‑admission series but also highlighted the Yankees’ reliance on late‑inning heroics. With the Dodgers still reeling from an 8‑2 defeat in the opener, the game underscored the volatility of bullpen matchups. Fans and analysts will be watching how both clubs adjust their rosters moving forward.

## What happened
In the first game of the doubleheader, the Dodgers dominated with an 8‑2 victory, propelled by a complete‑game effort from Yoshinobu Yamamoto. The Yankees managed only a single RBI from Cody Bellinger in the opening inning, leaving them trailing heavily.

The nightcap remained tight. Cody Bellinger delivered a first‑inning RBI single, the only run for New York until the eighth. Dodgers’ Tommy Edman answered with a pinch‑hit, game‑tying homer, knotting the score at 1‑1.

In the eighth, Jazz Chisholm Jr. took an Evan Phillips fastball to the opposite field for his 14th home run of the season, breaking the tie and securing a 2‑1 win for the Yankees. Reliever Fernando Cruz’s first pitch was delayed due to a glove issue, but the Yankees capitalized on the opportunity.

## Why it matters
