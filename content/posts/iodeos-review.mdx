The smartphone landscape is dominated by Android and iOS, posing a threat to national security due to dependence on two American tech giants. In Europe, there's a growing realization of the need for alternative solutions. One such alternative is iodéOS, a de-Googled Android platform. 
<Callout type="takeaway">iodéOS offers a frictionless de-Googled Android experience with microG and the Aurora Store.</Callout>
## What happened
IodéOS is based on the Android Open Source Project, with all Google services removed and replaced with alternative services and applications. The company behind iodéOS, also called iodé, is based in France and offers devices with iodéOS preinstalled or as a ROM for supported devices. 
IodéOS uses the LineageOS route with microG installed on top, which replaces proprietary parts of Google Play Services with open source reimplementations. 
The microG installation is updated through a dedicated F-Droid repository.

## Why it matters
The use of iodéOS and similar de-Googled Android variants can reduce dependence on American tech giants and improve national security. 
It also provides an alternative for users who want more control over their data and devices. 
However, the lack of certain applications and services may be a drawback for some users.
