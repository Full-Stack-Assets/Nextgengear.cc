xTool, a company primarily known for its laser cutting technology, has unveiled its latest innovation: the 01 Omni Printer. This new device steps firmly into the world of digital-to-physical creation, claiming to be the "world's first 4-in-1 printer" by natively combining UV, Direct-to-Garment (DTG), Direct-to-Film (DTF), and UV DTF technologies. The 01 Omni Printer aims to offer makers unprecedented versatility, capable of producing vivid, detailed, and even textured output on almost any imaginable surface, with relief up to 5mm thick.

<Callout type="takeaway">
The xTool 01 Omni Printer unifies multiple printing technologies into a single desktop device, offering makers unprecedented versatility for customized, textured output on diverse materials.
</Callout>

## What happened
xTool launched the 01 Omni Printer at a special event, positioning it as a comprehensive solution for makers. The device integrates four distinct printing methods: UV printing for rigid surfaces, DTG for direct fabric customization, DTF for transfer creation, and UV DTF for durable transfers on various items. Pricing for the 01 Omni Printer starts at $1,699 for the single UV edition, with more advanced configurations reaching up to $2,799 for the UV and DT Fabric Edition.

The printer leverages xTool's established xTool Studio software, which is lauded for its user-friendliness, extensive online templates, and built-in AI generators for designs and logos. Demonstrations showcased the printer's ability to work across a vast range of materials, including wood, acrylic, glass, and metal, as well as garments. It also highlighted its capability for creating textured 2.5D prints with up to 5mm of relief, using a CMYKWV (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black, White, Varnish) ink process. Notably, xTool claims its UV inks are significantly more affordable than some competitors and produce less odor.

## Why it matters
The introduction of the 01 Omni Printer is a significant development for the maker community, as it consolidates several specialized printing machines into a single, compact desktop unit. This integration can lead to substantial savings in both cost and workspace for hobbyists and small businesses looking to diversify their creative output. The ability to print on virtually any surface, combined with the option for textured 2.5D effects, opens up new avenues for product customization and artistic expression that were previously more complex or expensive to achieve.

Furthermore, xTool's emphasis on lower ink costs and reduced odor addresses common pain points in the UV printing sector, potentially making advanced printing technologies more accessible and pleasant to use. The seamless integration with the xTool Studio software, which also supports other xTool machines like laser cutters, streamlines workflows and enhances efficiency for creators already invested in the xTool ecosystem.
