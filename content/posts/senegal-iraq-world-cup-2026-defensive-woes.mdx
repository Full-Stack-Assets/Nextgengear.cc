Senegal, despite showing flashes of brilliance in previous matches against France and Norway, has struggled significantly with defensive lapses, leaving them with little to show for their efforts. This pivotal encounter sees the Lions of Teranga needing a strong performance to secure their path to the last 32, while Iraq is determined to make history by earning their first-ever World Cup point. The stage is set for a compelling battle where both teams face immense pressure to deliver.

## What happened
Senegal has displayed a concerning pattern of defensive vulnerability in their World Cup campaign, despite moments of strong attacking play, particularly against France and Norway. This defensive "shambolic" performance, as described by analysts, has undermined their potential. Adding to their challenges, first-choice goalkeeper Édouard Mendy is sidelined due to an injury sustained against Norway, and experienced defender Kalidou Koulibaly, who admitted to a "howler" against Norway, starts on the bench.

Iraq, making only their second World Cup appearance after their debut in 1986, is chasing their inaugural point in the tournament. Their previous involvement saw them lose all three group matches. This match marks the first time Senegal and Iraq have ever played each other, adding a unique historical dimension to the encounter. Both teams are entering the match without having kept a clean sheet in their respective campaigns so far, though they have faced formidable opponents like Erling Haaland and Kylian Mbappé.
