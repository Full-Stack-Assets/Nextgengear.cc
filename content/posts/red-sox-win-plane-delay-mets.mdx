A routine pre‑game flight for the Boston Red Sox hit a snag on Tuesday, forcing the team’s arrival at Citi Field to be delayed. The plane’s mechanical issue pushed back the series‑opening start against the New York Mets, compressing the game’s schedule. Despite the disruption, Boston rallied and secured a win, giving the Sox an early edge in the three‑game set. The delay highlighted the fragile logistics that underlie even the most polished MLB events.

## What happened
Boston’s charter flight experienced a technical problem that required an unscheduled maintenance check, delaying the team’s arrival at the stadium. As a result, the game’s kickoff was pushed back, and the broadcast schedule was adjusted.

When the Sox finally took the field, they capitalized on early scoring opportunities and held off the Mets to finish with a win.

## Why it matters
The win not only gave Boston momentum heading into the series but also underscored how travel hiccups can ripple into on‑field performance. Teams that adapt quickly to logistical setbacks can preserve competitive balance, while fans and broadcasters must adjust expectations in real time.
