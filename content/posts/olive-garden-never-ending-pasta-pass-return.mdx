Olive Garden has announced the comeback of its beloved Never-Ending Pasta Pass, a promotion that was last available in 2019. Starting at 2 p.m. ET on July 16, the chain will sell 10,000 passes for $100 each, granting diners 13 weeks of unlimited pasta, sauces, protein toppings, soup, salad and breadsticks. The limited‑time offer arrives as Darden Restaurants faces pressure from higher input costs and a modest earnings outlook. For loyal guests, the pass is a chance to indulge without worrying about the bill, while the company hopes the buzz will drive foot traffic. Industry observers will watch whether the promotion can offset the chain’s recent sales slowdown.
<Callout type="takeaway">The revived Pasta Pass gives diners a low‑cost way to eat unlimited meals, while Olive Garden hopes it revives traffic amid earnings pressure.</Callout>
## What happened
Olive Garden announced on July 16 that it will release 10,000 Never-Ending Pasta Passes at a price of $100 plus tax. Each pass provides 13 weeks of unlimited pasta, sauces, protein toppings, as well as unlimited soup, salad and breadsticks for in‑restaurant dining.
The pass originally debuted in 2014 and was last offered in 2019. It cannot be redeemed for to‑go orders, and the promotion ends once all 10,000 passes are claimed.
The move comes as Darden Restaurants, Olive Garden’s parent, projected full‑year earnings per share between $11.10 and $11.35, slightly below analyst expectations, after reporting fourth‑quarter sales of $3.72 billion, just under the $3.73 billion forecast.
## Why it matters
For consumers, the pass represents a predictable dining cost and an incentive to visit Olive Garden more frequently. For the chain, the limited‑run promotion is designed to boost foot traffic, generate media buzz, and potentially improve same‑restaurant sales growth, which Darden expects to rise 2.5%‑3.5% this year. Competitors may feel pressure to launch similar value‑driven offers, while investors will watch whether the initiative helps close the earnings gap.

## How to think about it
Consider how often you eat at Olive Garden and whether you can realistically use the pass for at least 13 weeks of meals. If you plan to visit multiple times per week, the $100 cost quickly pays for itself. Budget the expense as a prepaid dining fund and track your visits to ensure you extract maximum value before the pass expires or sells out.
