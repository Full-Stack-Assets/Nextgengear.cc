Jasprit Bumrah was ruled out of the series‑deciding third ODI between India and England at Lord's just an hour before the toss. The fast bowler’s sudden knee problem forced the Indian camp to reshuffle its pace attack. Arshdeep Singh, along with Prince Yadav and KL Rahul, were drafted in as replacements. The match is pivotal as the series stands level at 1‑1, with both sides eager to claim the win. India now faces the challenge of compensating for the loss of its strike bowler in the final showdown.

## What happened
India’s lineup underwent three changes after Bumrah’s injury was confirmed. Arshdeep Singh, Prince Yadav and KL Rahul entered the squad, replacing Bumrah, Washington Sundar and Shivam Dube. The decision was announced by captain Shubman Gill at the toss, emphasizing the need for fresh wickets in the middle overs.

The series is tied 1‑1, with England having bounced back from a loss in the opening game. Both teams are now set to battle for the series win at the historic Lord's Cricket Ground.

## Why it matters
Bumrah’s absence removes India’s premier death‑over specialist, potentially weakening their ability to contain England’s scoring in the final overs. Arshdeep Singh, while promising, lacks Bumrah’s experience in high‑pressure situations, which could shift the balance toward England. The outcome will influence team morale ahead of the upcoming ODI World Cup and may affect selection decisions for future tours.
