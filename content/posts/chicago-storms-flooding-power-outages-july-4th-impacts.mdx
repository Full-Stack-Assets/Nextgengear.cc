Overnight storms unleashed a torrent of rain across the Chicago area, causing extensive flooding, widespread power outages, and tragically, a fatality. The severe weather led to significant road closures, numerous stranded vehicles, and the postponement of several Fourth of July celebrations. This event underscores the immediate dangers of extreme weather and its profound disruption to daily life and holiday plans.

## What happened

Starting Saturday morning, a Flood Warning was issued for many Chicago-area counties as significant rainfall led to widespread flooding. Roads across numerous suburbs, including Naperville, Elgin, and Bartlett, became impassable, leaving multiple vehicles stranded in high water. The Bishop Ford Freeway on the South Side was also closed in both directions for most of the day due to flooding.

Beyond road closures, the storms knocked out power for tens of thousands of customers across the region. In south suburban areas like Dolton and Harvey, residents reported flooded basements, and several motorists required rescue after their cars became stuck in high standing water, underscoring the immediate dangers posed by the conditions.

Tragically, a 47-year-old woman and her two dogs died in Kendall County after a tree fell on their camper trailer. The severe weather also impacted holiday plans, forcing the postponement of some Fourth of July events and even delaying the Cubs-Cardinals game at Wrigley Field.

## Why it matters

The recent storms in the Chicago area highlight the significant and multifaceted impacts of extreme weather on urban and suburban communities. Beyond the immediate inconvenience of power outages and traffic disruptions, the event posed serious safety risks, tragically evidenced by the fatality and the numerous vehicle rescues required across the region. The widespread nature of the flooding demonstrates the vulnerability of infrastructure to heavy rainfall, affecting transportation networks and residential properties alike.

Furthermore, the cancellation and postponement of holiday events like Fourth of July celebrations and sports games affect community morale and local economies. This incident serves as a stark reminder of the critical importance of public safety adherence, emergency preparedness, and robust infrastructure to mitigate the consequences of increasingly frequent and intense weather phenomena.

## How to think about it

When confronted with severe weather, particularly heavy rainfall and flood warnings, residents must prioritize safety above all else. The most critical guidance is to never attempt to drive through standing water. Floodwaters can be deceptively deep, conceal hidden hazards, and even shallow depths can stall a vehicle or sweep it away, putting lives at extreme risk. Always heed official warnings, turn around, and find an alternate route. Additionally, ensure you have an emergency plan in place, including knowing how to receive weather alerts, identifying safe routes, and having backup power options, to minimize disruption and ensure well-being when extreme weather strikes.
