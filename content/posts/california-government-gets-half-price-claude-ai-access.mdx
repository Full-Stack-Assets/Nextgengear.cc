California Governor Gavin Newsom has announced a significant partnership with AI company Anthropic, securing a 50% discount for the state government on access to Anthropic's Claude AI. This deal also includes free workforce training and expert technical assistance for state and local agencies. The initiative aims to integrate advanced AI capabilities responsibly into public service, improving efficiency, cybersecurity, and citizen engagement across various departments. This move positions California at the forefront of AI adoption in government, leveraging its strong tech industry ties to enhance public sector operations.

<Callout type="takeaway">California's government has secured a substantial discount and support package for Anthropic's Claude AI to modernize public services and operations.</Callout>

## What happened
Governor Newsom's office announced the partnership, granting the California state government, along with city and county levels, a 50% discount on Anthropic's Claude AI. Beyond cost savings, the agreement includes free workforce training, expert GenAI technical assistance, and workflow input directly from Anthropic developers. This comprehensive package is designed to facilitate the responsible and effective integration of AI into government functions.

The technology will primarily be used for tasks such as drafting, summarization, and analysis, supplementing day-to-day work and improving services for Californians. Access to Claude will be centralized through the new Statewide Information Technology Shared Services (SITeS) portal. Specific integrations are already underway, including the Engaged California tool for policymaking input, and enhancing cybersecurity with Claude Security and Claude Code at the California Department of Technology and the Governor's Office of Emergency Services.

Furthermore, the California DMV plans to utilize Claude to reduce wait times and enhance services, while the Department of Healthcare Services will deploy it to assist with the state's Medicaid program. Anthropic, a California-based company, expressed a sense of responsibility to its home state, emphasizing its commitment to building AI responsibly and in service of people, aligning with Governor Newsom's vision for the technology's deployment.

## Why it matters
This partnership signifies a major step for California in leveraging advanced AI to streamline government operations and improve citizen services. By securing a significant discount and comprehensive support, the state is making cutting-edge AI accessible to agencies that might otherwise face budgetary constraints, including local governments. This could set a precedent for other states and public sectors looking to adopt AI responsibly, demonstrating a model for collaboration between government and leading tech companies. The focus on "responsible, transparent, and in service of people" highlights a commitment to ethical AI deployment, aiming to augment human work rather than replace it, which is crucial for public trust and worker acceptance. The integration into critical areas like cybersecurity, healthcare, and DMV services has the potential to yield tangible benefits, from faster response times to more efficient program management.
