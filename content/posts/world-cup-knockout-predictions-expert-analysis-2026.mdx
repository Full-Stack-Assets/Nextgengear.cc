After 72 thrilling group stage matches, the World Cup knockout bracket is officially set, leaving 32 teams vying for the sport's most coveted prize. With host nations still in contention and superstars like Lionel Messi, Kylian Mbappe, Erling Haaland, and Harry Kane delivering stunning performances, anticipation for the next five rounds is at an all-time high. The Athletic's writers have shared their insights, offering a fascinating look into who they believe will emerge victorious, which teams are captivating audiences, and where the biggest shocks might occur. Their collective wisdom provides a compelling framework for understanding the narratives that will define this tournament.

<Callout type="takeaway">Experts largely favor France as the strongest team, with Japan emerging as a captivating dark horse, while Lionel Messi continues to impress as the tournament's best player.</Callout>

## What happened
Journalists from The Athletic have cast their votes on key questions surrounding the World Cup knockout stages. France emerged as the overwhelming favorite for the "best team to watch," with multiple writers praising their quality and dynamic play, especially after a strong showing post-opening game. Ousmane Dembele's hat-trick against Norway was cited as evidence of their formidable attack alongside Kylian Mbappe.

Beyond France, several other nations garnered significant attention. Japan was frequently mentioned for their "mesmerising" and fluent play, despite missing key players, showcasing a true team effort. The USA, under Mauricio Pochettino, also surprised many with their "dynamic, intense vertical football," making their games must-see. Other teams like Morocco, Argentina (led by a 39-year-old Messi), and Colombia were also highlighted for their invention, ambition, and emotional drive.

When it came to the "best player," Lionel Messi was the clear consensus, with many acknowledging his continued brilliance despite his age. Kylian Mbappe and Vinicius Junior also received nods for their impactful performances, but Messi's enduring quality on the pitch was deemed undeniable by the majority of experts.

## Why it matters
The expert predictions offer a valuable lens through which fans can approach the unpredictable nature of knockout football. By highlighting both established powerhouses like France and surprising, aesthetically pleasing teams like Japan and the USA, these analyses shape expectations and narratives for the remainder of the tournament. They underscore the evolving landscape of international football, where tactical innovation and collective spirit can challenge traditional giants.

Furthermore, the consensus around Lionel Messi as the best player, even at 39, reinforces his legendary status and the sheer impact he continues to have on the game. These insights not only fuel fan discussions and betting markets but also provide a framework for appreciating the diverse styles and individual brilliance on display, making every match a potential spectacle of upsets or expected dominance.
