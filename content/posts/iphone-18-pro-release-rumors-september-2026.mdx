Apple is gearing up for another annual showcase, this time promising the iPhone 18 family—including the standard 18, the 18 Pro, and a rumored foldable iPhone Ultra. A recent massive hack leaked internal supplier documents, giving the rumor mill fresh fuel just weeks before the expected September event. The launch will also debut iOS 27, marking the next step in Apple's software evolution. All eyes are on new CEO John Ternus, who will take the stage on Sept. 1, as analysts warn that ongoing RAM shortages could push prices higher.

<Callout type="takeaway">The iPhone 18 series is set for a September 2026 debut, but buyers should brace for steeper prices. </Callout>

## What happened
Apple's flagship lineup is expected to arrive later this year, featuring the iPhone 18, iPhone 18 Pro, and the first‑ever iPhone Ultra foldable, according to multiple industry trackers. The leak frenzy intensified after a hack exposed thousands of internal documents, confirming that iOS 27 is in development and that the 18 Pro will use more expensive memory chips, costing Apple about $150 more per unit than the 17 Pro.

The company’s September event has been a staple for over a decade, and this year it will be the first under new CEO John Ternus, whose term began on Sept. 1. Tim Cook previously warned that “price increases are unavoidable,” citing the RAM shortage that is inflating component costs across smartphones, laptops, and consoles.

## Why it matters
Higher component costs mean the iPhone 18 Pro will likely launch at a premium, squeezing budget‑conscious consumers and potentially widening the gap between Apple and its competitors. The introduction of a foldable model could reshape the premium market, but supply‑chain constraints may limit early availability. For developers, iOS 27 promises new APIs that could accelerate AI‑driven apps, influencing the broader app ecosystem.
